# - ⚽📊 **English Championship 2025/26 – Market Efficiency Analysis (Over/Under 2.5 Goals)**

I built a football analytics project to evaluate how well betting markets price goal intensity in the English Championship.

Instead of focusing on match results alone, I asked a more interesting question:

> Do bookmakers accurately predict how many goals teams actually produce?

---

## 🎯 Objective

To measure whether Championship teams **overperform or underperform the Over/Under 2.5 goals market expectations** using Bet365 closing odds.

---

## 📊 Data Used

* English Championship 2025/26 match data
* Full Time Goals (FTHG, FTAG)
* Full Time Results (FTR)
* Bet365 Over/Under 2.5 odds
* Derived market probabilities

---

## 🔍 Key Findings

* Several teams consistently **produce more goals than the market expects**

  * QPR
  * Sheffield United
  * Stoke

* Other teams consistently **produce fewer goals than expected**

  * Derby
  * Middlesbrough
  * Southampton
  * Blackburn

👉 This shows that betting markets can misprice **team-level goal behavior patterns**

---

## 📈 Visuals Built

* Plotly bar charts for team over/underperformance
* Diverging league-wide efficiency map
* Calibration table (Expected vs Actual Over 2.5 rates)
* Ranked team efficiency dashboard

---

## ⚽ Tactical Implications

These patterns reflect deeper football structure:

* High overperformance teams → open play, transitions, defensive instability
* Low overperformance teams → structured defense, controlled tempo

👉 Tactical identity influences how often matches exceed market goal expectations.

---

## 🚀 Actionable Recommendations

This framework can be applied to:

* Sports betting value identification (Over/Under markets)
* Predictive football modeling
* Tactical team profiling
* Market inefficiency detection systems
* Feature engineering for machine learning models

---

## 📌 Final Insight

The betting market is efficient overall…

But at team level, **systematic goal patterns emerge that can be measured and analyzed.**
